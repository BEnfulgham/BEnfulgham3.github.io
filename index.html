About Me

My name is Benjamin Fulgham, I am a sophomore in the University of Kentucky's chemical engineering program. I have had an internship at Wacker chemical in Calvert City since July of 2021 where I am able to blend my education with real world experience. While studying at the University of Kentucky I have had the pleasure of being part of many community outreach events including Death by Chocolate, an event geared towards encouraging woman to pursue engineering. I have developed many skills this semester in Comp Tools including: using Aspen Plus, Excel, and Matlab as tools to aid in my engineering goals. Check out my work.

My Goal in engineering after I graduate is to become a production engineer. I enjoy working in production because every day can be different, yet every day is centered around quality, productivity, and safety. I enjoy finding the patterns that correlate to better quality and developing ways to make processes faster and more rewarding. I also want to keep all facility personal safe by following proper procedures and mending out dated ones. Making sure everyone goes home safe is part of the engineering code of ethics and is at the fore front of every decision I will make.

Work

Throughout the semester I was tasked with using Matlab to develop plots of data and to plot images, this is an image plotted in matlab of plots made in matlab. On the left is a plot of a theoretical rocket launch with the Y axis being the height of the rocket corresponding to the X axis time in seconds since lift off. On the right is a daily schedule shown in multiple ways to indicate the percent of time spent on daily activities. The daily schedule is plotted using the subplot function which allows a figure to show multiple graphs.

I also grow my understanding of methods to solve linear and non-linear systems of equations in Matlab and Excel. Above is an example of an non-linear system of equations solved using the Gaussian method.

In comp tools we were also exposed to new document creating software. In the image above is a resume I created using LaTeX. The program uses code to format a neat and well put together document that can be easily updated as I continue in my engineering career.

I also learned new features in Excel including macros and VBA programs. Above is a simple VBA program I wrote to calculate the missing side length of a right triangle using Pythagorean theorem. We use VBA programs a lot at my internship to simplify our quality spreadsheets. I definitely see the value in VBA's and will definitely use them in my career.

Aspen Plus is another tool I began to use while in Comp Tools. Above is a simple flow diagram of a flash separator that aspen calculated the heat duty, exiting flow, and composition of once I completed the necessary inputs. Aspen is a great tool that drastically reduces the tedious calculations of designing new processes and will be a handy tool on my belt in the future.
